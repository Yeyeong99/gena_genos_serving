## Student Volunteers' Gathering and Speeches

- **Section Scope**: Seoul Transition, Police Suppression, and Class Contrast (Pages 101–125).
- **Narrative Arc & Flow**:
  - **Seoul Establishment & Threat (pp. 101–110)**: Yeong-shin returns to Seoul (Cheongseok-dong), transforming a rented room into a free clinic/night school ('Chae Seonsaeng'). Despite growing popularity treating neighbors and teaching literacy, she faces colonial surveillance. A police officer summons her, citing 'overcrowding' and 'unauthorized fundraising,' threatening closure unless class size is reduced from 100 to 50.
  - **Expulsion & Despair (pp. 111–115)**: Enforcement of the police order triggers chaos. Excluded children cling to Yeong-shin, weeping, while church staff forcibly drag them away. Yeong-shin is left teaching the remaining 50 students outdoors through a window, with the excluded children watching helplessly from the wall. The scene shifts from frantic heartbreak to quiet despair.
  - **Sacrifice & Warning (pp. 116–120)**: Resolving to build a new school, Yeong-shin embarks on a desperate fundraising campaign in remote villages. She endures severe hunger, exhaustion, and physical deterioration. Dong-hyeok's letter arrives, warning her not to sacrifice her health, highlighting the tension between her mission and physical limits.
  - **Grotesque Contrast (pp. 121–125)**: Sharp ironic shift to Heukseok-ri, where usurer Han Nak-cheong hosts a lavish birthday party featuring geisha, music, and excessive drinking. Yeong-shin arrives with children, mistaken for guests. In an awkward confrontation masking underlying tension, Han offers food which she refuses. The scene juxtaposes her gritty suffering against the elite's vulgar opulence.

- **Discourse State & Open References**:
  - **Current Status**: Night school is crippled/split; outdoor teaching is a temporary, inadequate fix. Yeong-shin's health is critically declining. The goal of building a new school is urgent but financially impossible. Han Nak-cheong is established as a primary antagonist symbolizing local corruption and moral decay. Relationship with Dong-hyeok is strained by her self-sacrifice.
  - **Unresolved Conflicts**: Will the clinic/school be permanently shut down? Can Yeong-shin sustain her health amidst fundraising failures? How will the tension with Han Nak-cheong escalate after the party intrusion? Can the 'three-year promise' survive her physical collapse? Will the excluded children return?
  - **Key References**: Police order (50 student limit); Outdoor window teaching setup; Han Nak-cheong (usurer, Heukseok-ri); Lavish birthday party (geisha, alcohol); Yeong-shin's physical exhaustion/hunger; Dong-hyeok's warning letter; Fundraising failure in remote villages; Awkward party intrusion/refusal of food; 'Chae Seonsaeng' role; Cheongseok-dong location.

- **Tone, Register & Style Continuity**:
  - **Register Shifts**: Frantic/heartbreaking tone during the expulsion scene transitions to determined/weary during fundraising, then to satirical/disgusted during the party description. Yeong-shin's external speech remains formal/polite even in distress, contrasting with raw, strained internal monologues. Han Nak-cheong and geisha use colloquial, rough dialect to emphasize vulgarity.
  - **Descriptive Style**: Sensory focus on tears, dust, and hunger pangs (Yeong-shin) vs. smell of alcohol, noise of music, and greasy food (Party). Abrupt sentence structures capture the chaos of expulsion and the shock of the class contrast. Flowing structures return only for Yeong-shin's internal resolve. The narrative rhythm emphasizes the sharp irony between the educator's sacrifice and the usurer's waste.
