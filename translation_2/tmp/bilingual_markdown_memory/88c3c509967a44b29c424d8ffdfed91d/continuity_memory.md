**Narrative Arc & Progression**
The narrative resolves the 'vocation vs. love' tension through a decisive beach confession. Yeong-sin reveals a pre-arranged engagement to Kim Yeong-geun (a materialistic financier), explicitly rejects this 'feudal' match, and chooses Dong-hyeok. The 'protective distance' motif shatters as they share their first physical intimacy (embrace/kiss), transitioning from suspended tension to acknowledged mutual love. However, immediate separation looms: they agree to a 'three-year plan' to solidify their rural work before marriage. 

Yeong-sin returns to Cheongseokgol, formally breaking the engagement via letter (shifting honorifics from formal 'jeon' to intimate 'ae'), facing her mother's grief and the fiancé's threats. Her role expands from teacher to de facto village doctor and fundraiser amidst severe resource scarcity. The school grows to 130+ students, overflowing the chapel, until police intervention imposes a strict cap of 80 students, warning against 'coercive' fundraising. Yeong-sin is forced to traumatically dismiss 50+ children using a 'chalk line' method; she teaches the expelled students through the window while they weep outside. 

Determined to build a new hall, Yeong-sin embarks on a grueling fundraising campaign, suffering heatstroke and humiliation from hostile wealthy landlords, specifically Han Nang-cheong. The arc culminates in a sharp tonal shift to Han Nang-cheong's opulent 60th birthday banquet, attended by Japanese officials and local elites. The scene contrasts grotesque excess (food, music, colonial subservience) with rural poverty, ending as Yeong-sin and the expelled children arrive at the banquet gates, setting up an imminent confrontation between the impoverished activists and the colonial-aligned elite.

*   **Central Tension:** Shifted from internal romantic conflict to external state suppression and class warfare. The 'three-year pact' delays marriage but enforces physical separation. The conflict is now 'grassroots survival vs. colonial/bureaucratic oppression' and 'impoverished education vs. elite excess.'
*   **Relationship Status:** Dong-hyeok and Yeong-sin are confirmed romantic partners and comrades. The 'protective distance' is broken, replaced by a disciplined delay strategy. Yeong-sin stands alone in Cheongseokgol, isolated by duty and the broken engagement.
*   **Atmosphere:** High contrast between lyrical romantic resolution, tragic intimacy (classroom expulsion), gritty physical suffering (fundraising collapse), and satirical grotesquerie (banquet excess). Sensory details anchor the political stakes: chalk lines, weeping children, heatstroke, lavish food, and colonial honorifics.

**Character Registers & Voices**
